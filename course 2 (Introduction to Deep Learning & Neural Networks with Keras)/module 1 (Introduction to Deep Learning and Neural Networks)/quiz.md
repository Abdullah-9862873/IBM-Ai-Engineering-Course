## Question 4 (Practice Quiz)
A neuron receives two float inputs: x1 = 0.3, x2 = 0.7. The corresponding weights are w1 = 0.2, w2 = 0.4, and the bias is b = 0.1. What is the value of the weighted sum z?

**Answer: 0.440**
- Calculation: z = (0.3 × 0.2) + (0.7 × 0.4) + 0.1 = 0.06 + 0.28 + 0.1 = 0.44

## Question 5 (Practice Quiz)
A neuron generates a weighted sum z = -0.4 in a deep learning model. Using the sigmoid activation function σ(z) = 1/(1 + e^(-z)), determine the activated output.

**Answer: 0.401**
- Calculation: σ(-0.4) = 1/(1 + e^0.4) = 1/(1 + 1.4918) = 0.401

## Question 7 (Practice Quiz)
An AI engineer is building a model based on the biological neuron structure. How does the information typically flow in an artificial neural network?

**Answer:** Electrical signals are first received by dendrites and sent to the soma for processing. The soma forwards the signals through the axon to the synapses, which transmit the output to neighboring neurons.

## Question 10 (Practice Quiz)
